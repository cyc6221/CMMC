Square-Root Barrier

The “square-root barrier” is the open problem of obtaining tight (no √-loss) security reductions for Fiat–Shamir signatures (e.g., Schnorr/GQ). Standard rewinding/forking proofs often lose a √ factor because extraction needs two accepting transcripts.

  • Paper Lower Bounds for Discrete Logarithms and Related Problems Victor Shoup. EUROCRYPT 1997.
  • Paper GQ and Schnorr Identification Schemes: Proofs of Security against Impersonation under Active and Concurrent Attacks Mihir Bellare and Adriana Palacio. CRYPTO 2002.
  • Paper The One-More-RSA-Inversion Problems and the Security of Chaum's Blind Signature Scheme Mihir Bellare, Chanathip Namprempre, David Pointcheval, and Michael Semanko. Journal of Cryptology, 2003.
  • Paper The Multi-Base Discrete Logarithm Problem: Tight Reductions and Non-Rewinding Proofs for Schnorr Identification and Signature Mihir Bellare and Wei Dai. INDOCRYPT 2020.
  • Paper The One-More Discrete Logarithm Assumption in the Generic Group Model Balthazar Bauer, Georg Fuchsbauer, and Antoine Plouviez.
  • Paper Tighter Security for Schnorr Identification and Signature: A High-Moment Forking Lemma for $\Sigma$-Protocols Lior Rotem and Gil Segev. Journal of Cryptology, July 2024.
  • Paper Tight Bounds on Uniform-Challenge Reductions from Sigma Protocols via Hitting Games Iftach Haitner and Nikolaos Makriyannis.
